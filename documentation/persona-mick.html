Persona

Name:

Mick Muller

Devices:

  • Has a laptop running Windows 11 and uses Google Chrome as his browser of choice. He doesn't know the exact specs but it's good enough to join online lobbies/sessions with his friends.
  • Has iphone model from 2 years ago and is comfortable with it for basic phone functions and for browsing, which he defaults to with Safari
  • Owns a Nintendo Switch for some casual and party games

Loves/Hates:

  • Loves gaming as a group activity with friends or in friendly social situations.
  • Finds some of his friends' enthusiasm for gaming infectious, but he tends to be one of the early ones to bed if a session starts going late into the night
  • Hates being too competitive or being hopelessly outmatched against clearly better players.
  • Hates the similar things that his friends do. If his friends are unhappy with whatever part or feature, he's unhappy about it with them.

Proficiency:

  • Mick is not the most tech-savvy, but doesn't mean he's completely clueless.
  • Mick is a relatively casual user who can figure his way around most apps he uses just for the express purposes of his own use. Don't test him on it though.

Attitudes and Behaviors:

  • Mick finds it easy to at least start conversations and make small talk with just about anyone.
  • He tends to be happy when everyone else (he cares about) is happy.
  • If he were to categorize himself as a gamer, it would be casual-core. He casually plays all the games his friends play hardcore and has fun just laughing off whatever happens, win or lose.
  • Easily worked up by others' genuine hype, enthusiasm, and/or passion.

Frustrations/Needs:

  • Needs more venues/options for social game nights with friends/family
  • Frustrated by lack of centralized information about games, locations, and events for the kinds of games he and his friends want to play

Goals:

Mick wants more games (especially if it's interesting ones he never heard about before) and more places/opportunities to play those games with friends and any friendly strangers with similar interests.

User Story:

As a player, I want to quickly figure out whether this app can lead me to the kind of games, venues, and other players I would like to see.

Use Case:

Mick's friend Brett tells him about a new app called A Game Away he's on to find new games and meetups for board games. Brett wants Mick to join him in the next game he's hosting on Saturday afternoon. Mick is onboard and is also curious about the new app as well. But first, time to join Brett's game.

  • Pre-Condition: Mick has received a link to the app called A Game Away from Brett in his DMs and/or email and is at the home page.
  • Post-Condition: Mick is signed up on the app and has joined Brett's game on Saturday.

Interaction-Flow

  • Mick clicks into the search bar at the top
  • A Game Away app focuses on the search bar
  • Mick starts typing the name of the game Brett is hosting this weekend.
  • A Game Away app gives a dropdown of possible results on his inputs, narrowing the choices with each additional character typed in
  • Mick finds the game, he clicks on it
  • A Game Away App takes Mick to an information page about the selected game with accompanying text and images.
  • Just a little below on the same page, Mick notices a short list of upcoming sessions for the game. He can't find Brett's game in the short list, so he clicks on the 'Show All' button
  • A Game Away app then interrupts and prompts Mick to a simple sign-up form as requirement to use the create/join features of game sessions.
  • Mick fills in the form, submits.
  • The app displays a page that direct Mick to check the email he entered to validate and continue
  • Mick opens a tab for his email and finds the recently sent email for validation link. He clicks it
  • The app confirms his validation then automatically, then it redirects Mick to log-in form
  • Mick logs in and the app directs him back to where he was before sign-up/log-in and displays the full list extending towards the bottom of the page.
  • Mick scrolls down to find Brett's name as the host in a session and clicks on the session.
  • A Game Away app displays a new page with more detailed information on the planned game session including date, time, slots available, information on hosts and registered players to the session
  • Mick clicks on an empty slot available in the session
  • A Game Away app fills Mick's name and information into the session
